# MT Finance — Buy-to-Let Lending Policy (v13, April 2026)

> **Internal reference only. This document summarises MT Finance BTL Lending Policy v13 (April 2026). All lending decisions must be made in accordance with the full policy document.**

---

## §1 — Regulatory Framework

MT Finance BTL mortgages are advanced exclusively on a **non-regulated** basis. The policy covers:

- Properties let or to be let on a commercial basis where the borrower is not and will not be occupying the property as their main or only residence.
- Limited Company / SPV borrowers — always non-regulated regardless of property use history.

**Consumer BTL** (where the borrower or a close family member has previously occupied the property) is **not offered**. Applications identified as consumer BTL at any point must be declined.

| Trust | **No** |

### §4.2 Individual Applicant Criteria

- Minimum age at application: **21**
- Maximum age at end of term: **85** (Tier 1); **80** (Tier 2 — see §23)
- UK residents accepted. Foreign nationals with Indefinite Leave to Remain (ILR) accepted on standard terms.
- Foreign nationals without ILR: maximum **65% LTV**; Head of Underwriting referral required.
- Expats: minimum income requirement applies (see §32.2).

### §4.3 First-Time Landlords

A First-Time Landlord (FTL) is defined as an applicant with **fewer than 12 months** active BTL experience at the point of application. FTLs are subject to:
- Minimum income requirement (§32.2)
- Additional credit history scrutiny

## §14 — Policy Limits and Concessions Framework

### §14.1 Definitions

**Policy Limit *(L)***: A hard boundary in lending policy. Breaching a Policy Limit means the case **cannot proceed to Offer** unless the breach is formally approved by the Credit Committee and the relevant Stakeholder sign-off is obtained. Mandate holders below Credit Committee level cannot approve Policy Limit breaches.

**Concession *(M)***: A higher-risk but not absolute breach. Concessions may be approved by a mandate holder at the appropriate level (Level 4 or above, depending on the concession). Must be documented on the credit paper with full rationale.

### §14.2 Policy Limits — approval route

Any item marked *(L)* throughout this policy:
- Requires **Credit Committee approval** AND
- Requires **relevant Stakeholder sign-off** (e.g., Head of Risk, Chief Credit Officer depending on breach type)
- Mandate holders acting alone cannot approve *(L)* items

### §14.3 Concessions — approval route

Any item marked *(M)* throughout this policy:
- Requires **Level 4 mandate or above**
- Must be documented with full rationale and mitigant analysis on the credit paper
- Recurring concessions on the same case require escalation

## §16 — Security and Charges

- MT Finance requires a **First Legal Charge** over all security properties *(L)*.
- The charge must be registered at **HM Land Registry** before drawdown *(L)*.
- Second charges are not accepted as primary security on BTL products.
- Where a property is already charged to another lender, the existing charge must be redeemed from the MT Finance facility at completion (purchase) or the existing lender must consent to a ranking deed (rare; Head of Underwriting referral).

## §21 — Valuation Basis

- LTV is always calculated on the **lower of purchase price or valuation** (for purchases) or the **valuation alone** (for remortgages).
- A valuation from an MT Finance panel valuer is required for all cases.
- Desktop or AVM valuations are not accepted as the sole basis for LTV calculation on loans above £150,000.
- Reinspection required if more than **6 months** have elapsed since the original valuation report date at the point of offer.

## §23 — Obligor Exposure and LTV Tiers

Obligor exposure is the **total aggregate outstanding debt** to MT Finance across all facilities for the same borrower group (individual, company, or connected entities).

| Obligor Tier | Total MT Finance Exposure | Maximum LTV |
|---|---|---|
| Tier 1 | Up to and including £1,500,000 | 80% |
| Tier 1 (product-dependent) | Up to and including £1,500,000 | 75% on standard 2/5-yr Fixed products |
| Tier 2 | Above £1,500,000 | 75% |
| Tier 2 — additional restriction | Above £3,000,000 | 65% |

**Notes:**
- Once aggregate exposure exceeds £1,500,000, the maximum LTV on any new facility drops to **75%** *(L)*.
- Once aggregate exposure exceeds £3,000,000, the maximum LTV drops to **65%** *(L)*.
- These are hard limits. Exceeding them requires Credit Committee approval.

## §24 — Affordability: Interest Cover Ratio (ICR)

### §24.1 ICR Thresholds

The minimum ICR is assessed against the **stressed monthly interest cost**, not the pay rate.

| Borrower Type | Minimum ICR |
|---|---|
| Individual — basic-rate taxpayer | 125% |
| Individual — higher / additional-rate taxpayer | 145% |
| Limited Company / SPV / LLP | **125%** |
| HMO (any borrower type) | 125% |
| Multi-Unit Freehold Block (any borrower type) | 125% |

### §24.2 Stressed Interest Rate

The stressed interest rate is applied to the **loan amount** to calculate the notional annual interest cost, which is then compared to the **annual rental income**.

| Product Type | Borrower Category | Stressed Rate |
|---|---|---|
| Any fixed rate product with term **< 5 years** (e.g. 2-yr Fixed) — all borrowers | General (any) | **5.50%** |
| Any fixed rate product with term **≥ 5 years** (e.g. 5-yr Fixed) | General (any) | **5.00%** |
| Historic owner remortgage — £-for-£, no capital raising, property owned before 6 April 2017 | Personal name only | **5.00%** (regardless of product term) |
| Tracker / variable rate product | Any | **5.50%** |

> **Important — Historic Owner Exception:** The 5.00% rate for <5-year fixed products applies **only** to individual borrowers remortgaging a property they owned personally before 6 April 2017, where there is no additional capital raising. It does **not** apply to Limited Company / SPV borrowers, new purchases, or any case involving capital raising. Underwriters must not apply this exception without confirming all three conditions are met.

### §24.3 ICR Calculation

```
Annual Rental Income = Monthly Rent × 12
Annual Stressed Interest = Loan Amount × Stressed Rate
ICR = Annual Rental Income / Annual Stressed Interest × 100%
```

**Example (Ltd Co, 2-yr Fixed, purchase):**
- Loan: £214,200
- Stressed rate: 5.50% (§24.2 — <5-year fixed, all borrowers)
- Annual stressed interest: £214,200 × 5.50% = £11,781
- Rental income at stated rent (£1,200/month): £14,400/year → ICR = **122.2%** → **FAILS 125% minimum**
- Rental income at comp rent (£1,000/month): £12,000/year → ICR = **101.9%** → **FAILS 125% minimum**

### §24.4 Rental Income Evidence

- Where a tenancy is in place: rental income evidenced by valid tenancy agreement (see §43).
- Where no tenancy is in place at the time of application: the **valuer's market rent assessment** is the authoritative figure for ICR purposes. The applicant's stated rent may be used at DIP stage but must be confirmed by the valuer before Offer.
- In all cases, the ICR calculation at Offer must use the **lower of** stated rent and valuer's market rent assessment.

## §26 — Portfolio Landlords

### §26.1 Definition

A **Portfolio Landlord** is defined as an applicant (individual or company) with **4 or more mortgaged BTL properties** in the background portfolio at the point of application (including the subject property if the loan proceeds).

### §26.2 Portfolio Assessment Requirements

Portfolio landlords are subject to additional underwriting requirements:

- A **full portfolio schedule** must be provided, covering: address, estimated value, outstanding mortgage balance, monthly rental income, lender name, and product type for each property.
- A **portfolio-level affordability test** must be passed: aggregate portfolio rental income / aggregate portfolio stressed interest cost ≥ **125%**, using a stressed rate of **5.00%** across all properties regardless of individual product type.
- Maximum portfolio LTV for the background portfolio: **80%** (aggregate outstanding balances / aggregate values).
- If any property in the background portfolio is in arrears, a full explanation is required before the new application can proceed.

### §26.3 Portfolio Schedule

The portfolio schedule must be dated within **3 months** of the application date. A schedule older than 3 months must be refreshed before the case proceeds to Offer.

## §28 — Anti-Money Laundering and Source of Funds

### §28.1 Identity Verification

All applications require:
- **Two forms of ID** per individual (one photographic — passport or driving licence; one proof of address dated within 3 months)
- For SPV/Ltd Co: Companies House confirmation statement, share register, certificate of incorporation, and ID for all directors and all shareholders with ≥25% interest.

### §28.9 Source of Deposit / Source of Funds

- The source of any deposit or equity contribution must be fully evidenced.
- Acceptable sources: personal savings (6 months bank statements), gift (gift letter + donor ID + donor bank statements), sale of property (completion statement), business funds (business accounts + accountant's letter confirming legitimacy).
- Gift from a third party (non-family): Head of Underwriting referral required.
- Cash deposits with no clear audit trail: **not acceptable** *(L)*.
- Source of wealth is required for loans above £1,000,000 or where the deposit source appears unusual relative to the applicant's income profile.

## §30 — Limited Company and SPV Borrowers

### §30.1 Acceptable Company Types

- Special Purpose Vehicle (SPV) — registered in England and Wales, with BTL as a stated purpose in the articles *(L)*
- Trading Limited Company — acceptable subject to Head of Underwriting review
- Limited Liability Partnership (LLP) — acceptable

### §30.2 Unacceptable Company Types

- Partnership (non-LLP)
- Public Limited Company (PLC)
- Trust
- Offshore company or company incorporated outside England and Wales *(L)*

### §30.3 Registration Requirement

The company must be registered in **England and Wales** *(L)*. Registration in Scotland, Northern Ireland, or overseas is not acceptable. This must be confirmed from the Companies House confirmation statement before Offer.

> **§30.3a — Guarantor Requirements for Limited Company Borrowers (Hard Gate *(L)*)**
>
> For all Limited Company and LLP applications:
>
> 1. **All Directors** of the company must provide personal guarantees.
> 2. **All Shareholders holding ≥25% controlling interest** must also provide personal guarantees.
> 3. The guarantors in aggregate must hold a **≥60% controlling interest** in the company *(L)*.
> 4. A **Director's search** must be conducted on each proposed Guarantor. The Director's search must confirm:
>    - No directorship of a company that has been **liquidated in the last 3 years** *(L)*
>    - No current or historical **director disqualification** *(L)*
> 5. These are all hard gates. The case cannot proceed to Offer if any condition is not met or cannot be confirmed.
> 6. The confirmation statement must be reviewed to identify all shareholders with ≥25% interest. Reliance on the applicant's declaration alone is not sufficient.

### §30.4 SPV Accounts and Trading History

- SPVs are not required to have a trading history, but must have been incorporated before the date of the loan offer *(L)*.
- For trading companies (as opposed to pure SPVs), 2 years of accounts are required.
- SPV bank statements (minimum 3 months) must be provided where the SPV has been active. Where recently incorporated with no trading activity, a nil-activity bank statement or accountant's confirmation is acceptable.

## §31 — Credit History

### §31.1 Mortgage Arrears

| Arrears History | Tier 1 | Tier 2 |
|---|---|---|
| None in last 36 months | Acceptable | Acceptable |
| 1 × 1-month arrears in last 12 months | Acceptable | Not acceptable |
| 1 × 1-month in last 36 months | Acceptable | Acceptable with explanation |
| 2+ arrears in last 12 months | Not acceptable | Not acceptable |
| Any arrears in last 6 months | Not acceptable *(L)* | Not acceptable *(L)* |

### §31.2 Unsecured Arrears / Default

| History | Tier 1 | Tier 2 |
|---|---|---|
| Default registered in last 24 months | Not acceptable | Not acceptable |
| Default registered > 24 months ago (satisfied) | Acceptable with explanation | Not acceptable |
| Default registered > 24 months ago (unsatisfied) | Not acceptable | Not acceptable |

### §31.3 County Court Judgements (CCJs)

| CCJ History | Tier 1 | Tier 2 |
|---|---|---|
| No CCJs in last 36 months | Acceptable | Acceptable |
| 1 CCJ ≤ £500 in last 36 months (satisfied) | Acceptable | Not acceptable |
| 1 CCJ > £500 in last 36 months | Not acceptable | Not acceptable |
| Any CCJ unsatisfied | Not acceptable *(L)* | Not acceptable *(L)* |

### §31.4 Bankruptcy / IVA / Administration Orders

- Undischarged bankruptcy: **decline** *(L)*
- Discharged bankruptcy < 6 years ago: **not acceptable**
- Discharged bankruptcy ≥ 6 years ago: case-by-case at Head of Underwriting discretion
- IVA in last 6 years: not acceptable
- Debt Management Plan (DMP) currently active: not acceptable; if satisfied > 3 years ago, case-by-case

### §31.5 PEPs and Sanctions

- Any applicant, director, guarantor, or beneficial owner who is a **Politically Exposed Person (PEP)** requires **Enhanced Due Diligence** and **Senior Management sign-off** before the case can proceed.
- Any match to **Sanctions lists** (HM Treasury, OFAC, EU): **mandatory decline** *(L)*.
- ComplyAdvantage (or equivalent approved screening tool) screening is mandatory for all individuals before the case progresses past FMA stage.

### §31.6 Criminal Convictions

- Criminal convictions for **fraud, financial crime, or arson** result in a **mandatory decline**, even where the conviction is spent under the Rehabilitation of Offenders Act 1974 *(L)*.
- Other criminal convictions: refer to Head of Underwriting with full details.

### §31.7 Credit Score — Below Average

Where the credit bureau score for any applicant, director, or guarantor is rated **Below Average** by the relevant bureau, this constitutes a Concession *(M)* and requires a **Level 4 mandate or higher** for approval. The rationale and mitigants must be documented in full on the credit paper.

### §31.8 Payday Loans

- Any payday loan usage in the last **12 months**: not acceptable.
- Payday loan usage between **12 and 36 months** ago: Concession *(M)*; requires Level 4 mandate.
- No payday loan usage in last **36 months**: acceptable, but note on credit paper if within 48 months.

## §32 — Income Requirements

### §32.1 General

MT Finance does not impose a minimum personal income requirement on experienced landlords (≥12 months BTL experience) applying in personal name or through an established company.

### §32.2 Minimum Income — £15,000

A minimum **annual income of £15,000** per applicant (or per director/guarantor for Ltd Co applications) is required where the applicant falls into any of the following categories:

- **First-Time Landlords** (< 12 months BTL experience)
- **Expats** (UK nationals living/working overseas)
- Applicants with **< 12 months BTL experience** regardless of other criteria
- **Semi-commercial** applications
- **SPV / Ltd Co** applications where no director/guarantor has a track record of ≥12 months active BTL ownership

Income must be evidenced by:
- Employed: most recent 3 months' payslips + latest P60
- Self-employed: most recent 2 years' SA302 tax calculations + accountant's letter
- Company director: 2 years' accounts + most recent 2 years' SA302

### §32.3 Acceptable Income Sources

- PAYE / salary
- Self-employment income (net profit or salary + dividends from own company)
- Pension income
- Rental income from other properties (net of mortgage costs — cannot be used as the sole income source for minimum income test)
- Investment income where regular and evidenced

### §32.4 Unacceptable Income Sources for Minimum Income Test

- Benefits (other than Pension Credit)
- Child maintenance or child support payments
- Lottery or gambling winnings

## §43 — Tenancy Requirements

### §43.1 Assured Periodic Tenancies (APT) — RRA 2025

> **From 1 May 2026**, the Renters Rights Act 2025 (RRA 2025) has replaced Assured Shorthold Tenancies (ASTs) with **Assured Periodic Tenancies (APT)** in England. This change is in force as of the policy update date (May 2026).

For any tenancy agreement reviewed on or after **1 May 2026**:

- New tenancy agreements must comply with the **APT framework** under the RRA 2025. Standard legacy AST templates are no longer legally valid for new tenancies.
- Existing ASTs entered into before 1 May 2026 have automatically transitioned to APTs. The underwriter must confirm the tenancy has transitioned and that no legacy AST-specific clauses (e.g., fixed-term break-clause restrictions) are being relied upon.
- Tenancy documentation on file must be reviewed to ensure compliance. If the document presented is a legacy AST dated **before 1 May 2026**, confirm it has transitioned; if dated **on or after 1 May 2026**, it must be in APT form.

### §43.2 Acceptable Tenancy Types

| Tenancy Type | Acceptable |
|---|---|
| Assured Periodic Tenancy (APT) — RRA 2025 compliant | Yes |
| Existing AST (pre-1 May 2026, confirmed transitioned to APT) | Yes |
| Commercial lease (LTA 1954) — sections 24-28 excluded | Yes |
| Commercial lease (LTA 1954) — sections 24-28 **not** excluded | **Refer to Underwriter** |
| Regulated tenancy (pre-1989) | Not acceptable *(L)* |
| Rent-to-rent arrangement | Not acceptable *(L)* |
| Holiday let / serviced accommodation agreement | Not acceptable on standard BTL *(L)* |

### §43.3 Rental Guarantee Schemes

Rental guarantee schemes (whereby a third party guarantees the rental income irrespective of actual receipt from the tenant) are **not acceptable** *(L)*. The rental income used for ICR purposes must reflect actual market rent without guarantee enhancements.

### §43.4 No Tenancy in Place at Application

Where the property is vacant at application:
- The valuer's market rent assessment is used for ICR purposes (see §24.4).
- The applicant must confirm the letting intention and approximate expected letting date.
- Offer may be issued on this basis; however, if the property remains vacant at the point of drawdown for more than 6 months post-offer, the valuation must be reinspected.

## §44 — Rental Guarantee Schemes and Commercial Leases

- **Rental Guarantee Schemes are not acceptable** *(L)* — see §43.3.
- **Commercial Leases under the Landlord and Tenant Act 1954 (LTA 1954)** that have **not** excluded sections 24-28 (security of tenure provisions) must be **referred to Underwriting**. Where sections 24-28 have been excluded by court order or agreement, the commercial lease is acceptable on standard terms.

---

## §46 — Energy Performance Certificate (EPC)

- The minimum acceptable EPC rating for any security property is **E** *(L)*.
- An EPC rating of **F or G** results in a **mandatory decline** unless the applicant provides a credible improvement plan demonstrating the property will reach at least E prior to letting *(L)*.
- The EPC certificate must be sighted on file and must not have expired (EPC certificates are valid for 10 years from date of issue).
- Where no EPC is available (e.g., newly built property), an EPC must be commissioned and the result confirmed before Offer *(L)*.
- Government EPC reform: MT Finance will monitor changes to minimum EPC standards (the proposed increase to C by 2028 for new tenancies) and update policy accordingly. No underwriting concession for below-C properties is currently available.

---

## §47 — Flood Risk

- Properties with a **greater than 1-in-30-year flood risk** as assessed by the Environment Agency flood map: **mandatory decline** *(L)*.
- Properties with a 1-in-75 or 1-in-100 year flood risk: acceptable subject to confirmation that the property has appropriate flood insurance in place (evidenced at Offer stage).
- The valuer must comment on flood risk in the valuation report. Where the valuer's report flags flood risk, the underwriter must cross-reference the EA flood map.

## §48 — Property Criteria

### §48.1 Geography

- Security properties must be located in **England or Wales** *(L)*.
- Scotland, Northern Ireland, Isle of Man, Channel Islands: **not acceptable**.

### §48.2 Minimum Property Value

- Minimum property value: **£50,000** *(L)*.
- Properties below £50,000 value: mandatory decline regardless of LTV or loan amount.

### §48.3 Tenure

| Tenure | Acceptable | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Freehold — residential | Yes | Standard |
| Leasehold — residential | Yes | Minimum 70 years unexpired at point of application; minimum 30 years remaining at end of term *(L)* |
| Commonhold | Case-by-case | Refer to Underwriting |
| Flying freehold | Not acceptable *(L)* | Unless de minimis (< 5% of floor area) and fully insured |
| Share of freehold | Yes | Standard |

> Leasehold: where the unexpired lease term is below **85 years** at application, the case must be referred to the panel valuer for specific comment on saleability and value impact. Where below 70 years: not acceptable *(L)*.

### §48.4 Property Types — Acceptable

- Standard residential houses and flats (brick/stone/tile construction)
- New build (subject to builder's warranty — NHBC, Premier Guarantee, or equivalent — and any new build LTV restrictions per current product guide)
- Purpose-built flats
- Converted flats (subject to valuer's comments on conversion quality and fire safety)
- Houses in Multiple Occupation (HMO) — see §48.5
- Multi-Unit Freehold Blocks (MUFB) — see §48.6
- Semi-commercial (mixed residential/commercial) — see §48.7

### §48.5 HMO Rules

- Maximum **6 bedrooms** on standard product.
- HMOs with 7 or more bedrooms: require **Head of Underwriting** referral.
- A valid **HMO licence** (where required by the local authority) must be evidenced before Offer *(L)*.
- ICR minimum: 125% (same as residential — see §24.1).
- Valuation must be on rental investment basis (not vacant possession).

### §48.6 Multi-Unit Freehold Block (MUFB) Rules

- Maximum **6 units** on standard product.
- MUFBs with 7 or more units: require **Head of Underwriting** referral.
- All units must be assessed on a vacant possession basis in the valuation.
- ICR assessed on aggregate rental income from all units.
- Minimum ICR: 125%.

### §48.7 Semi-Commercial Properties

Semi-commercial (mixed-use) properties — where part of the security is in commercial use — are considered subject to:
- **Minimum income requirement applies** (£15,000 — §32.2)
- Maximum LTV: **70%**
- Commercial element must not exceed **40%** of total floor area; above 40% refers to commercial mortgage product.
- ICR threshold: **125%** assessed on residential rental income only (commercial income excluded from ICR).
- Head of Underwriting referral required.

## §49 — Unacceptable Property Types

The following property types are **not acceptable** as security on any MT Finance BTL product *(L)*:

- Ex-local authority flats above **5 storeys**
- Properties with cladding requiring an EWS1 certificate rated **B2 or C** (unsafe — remediation required)
- Properties with an active Japanese Knotweed issue within 7 metres of the main structure (unless treated with a 10-year insured warranty in place)
- Properties currently let to a **family member** of the borrower
- Properties subject to a **Pre-emption Right or Right of First Refusal** that would restrict MT Finance's ability to enforce security
- **Agricultural restrictions** or tied housing
- Properties in active or recent (last 12 months) **Compulsory Purchase Order** area
- **Studio flats below 30 sqm** internal floor area
- **Houseboats**, **mobile homes**, **park homes**
- Properties where the borrower's income is **derived from the security property** (other than rental income from a BTL letting) — e.g., operating a business from the premises

## Summary: Key Hard Gates (*(L)* Items) for BTL SPV Applications

The following are Policy Limits that must be confirmed clear before any Offer is issued:

1. First Legal Charge registered at Land Registry (§16)
2. LTV within product and obligor exposure limits (§22, §23)
3. ICR meets minimum 125% at correct stressed rate (§24)
4. Company registered in England and Wales (§30.3)
5. All Directors providing personal guarantees (§30.3a)
6. All ≥25% shareholders providing personal guarantees (§30.3a)
7. Aggregate guarantor controlling interest ≥60% (§30.3a)
8. Director's search completed per guarantor — no liquidation (last 3 years), no disqualification (§30.3a)
9. No active sanctions match (§31.5)
10. No fraud/financial crime/arson conviction (§31.6)
11. EPC rating E or above evidenced (§46)
12. Flood risk not exceeding 1-in-30-year (§47)
13. Property in England or Wales (§48.1)
14. Tenancy documentation compliant with RRA 2025 APT framework where applicable (§43)
15. No rental guarantee scheme in place (§43.3, §44)
